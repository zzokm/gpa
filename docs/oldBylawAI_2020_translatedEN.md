## 3. Vision, Mission, and Objectives of the AI Program

### Vision of the Artificial Intelligence Program

## 7. Academic Requirements and Regulations

The academic requirements and regulating rules for obtaining the Bachelor's degree in Computers and Artificial Intelligence are identical to those found in the approved core program regulations of 2018.

### Article (1): Study System
* **A-** Study in the offered programs is based on the Credit Hour system. The academic year is divided into two regular semesters, where the credit hour serves as the educational unit of measurement to determine the weight of each course.
* **B-** The Faculty Board may approve the organization of intensive summer sessions for certain courses based on proposals from the scientific departments and in accordance with the Faculty's capacities and circumstances.
* **C-** To obtain a Bachelor's degree, a student must successfully complete **one hundred and thirty-five credit hours (135 credits)** over at least eight regular semesters, divided into four academic levels.

### Article (2): Language of Instruction
* Study in the offered programs is conducted in Arabic and English, depending on the requirements of each individual course.

### Article (3): Academic Advising
* The Faculty assigns an academic advisor from among the faculty members to each group of students. The advisor handles academic orientation, helps students select and register for courses, and guides them throughout their study period.
* The academic advisor's opinion is consultative, and the student remains fully responsible for the courses they choose to register for based on their own preference.

### Article (4): Registration, Drop, and Add
* **A-** At the beginning of each semester, students register for their chosen courses via the Faculty website during the designated periods set by the administration prior to the start of classes, according to the following schedule:

| Week | Academic Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| First Week | Regular Student Registration for Courses |
| Second Week | Late Registration (Subject to a fine) |
| Third & Fourth Weeks | Withdrawal of Courses |

## 8. Registration Credit Hours and GPA Rules

* **B-** The Faculty Board determines the minimum and maximum number of students required to open registration for any course.
* **C-** Number of Registration Hours:

#### For Regular Semesters:
* The minimum number of credit hours for registration is **(9) hours**. This minimum may be waived if the remaining hours required for the student's graduation are less than this limit.
* Maximum credit hours allowed based on the Cumulative Grade Point Average (GPA) at the start of the semester:
    1. Students with a **GPA $\ge$ 2** can register for a maximum of **18 credit hours**.
    2. Students with a **1 $<$ GPA $<$ 2** can register for a maximum of **15 credit hours**.
    3. Students with a **GPA $\le$ 1** can register for a maximum of **12 credit hours**.
* The maximum registration limit can be increased up to **21 credit hours** under the following specific circumstances:
    * Level 4 students who hold a GPA $\ge$ 2 at the start of the semester.
    * Students of any academic level who hold a GPA $\ge$ 3 at the start of the semester.
    * Students wishing to register for a course in which they previously received an "Incomplete" (I) grade, provided their current GPA $\ge$ 2.

#### For the Summer Semester:
* The summer term is an intensive 7-week session where the weekly instructional hours for each course are doubled.
* The regular maximum workload allowed for students is **6 credit hours**.
* The maximum limit may be increased to **9 credit hours** solely to satisfy the student's graduation requirements.

## 9. Withdrawal, Attendance, and Absences

* **D-** Following the completion of registration, students may drop or add one or more courses during the official Drop/Add period set by the Faculty. This process is conducted in coordination with the academic advisor through the Faculty's portal.
* Students are permitted to study various courses and register for higher-level courses provided they have completed the designated prerequisites. No student may register for an advanced course without passing its prerequisite.

### Article (5): Course Withdrawal
* **A-** After registering for courses, a student may withdraw from one or more courses during the period specified in Article 4, provided that the remaining workload does not drop below the minimum credit hour requirement for a single semester. In this instance, the student is not considered to have failed the course and will receive a withdrawal grade of **"W"**.
* **B-** If a student withdraws from one or more courses after the specified deadline without a compelling excuse accepted by the Faculty Board, they will be given a failing grade of **"F"** in those courses.

### Article (6): Attendance, Absence, and Excuses
* **A-** Attendance in the program is mandatory, and distance/external enrollment is not permitted. The student monitoring process is subject to the terms and regulations defined by the Faculty administration.
* **B-** To be eligible to sit for the final examination, a student must maintain an attendance rate of no less than **75%** of the lectures and tutorials/labs in each course. If a student's absence exceeds **25%** without an acceptable excuse, the Faculty Board has the authority to bar them from taking the final exam. In such cases, the student receives a grade of "zero" in the final examination component.
* If the student presents a compelling excuse accepted by the Faculty Board within the designated timeframe, they will receive a withdrawal grade of "W" for that course.
* **C-** Any student who absents themselves from the final exam of any course without an approved excuse will receive a grade of "zero" in that exam, and must re-register and retake the entire course.
* If a student provides a valid medical or compelling excuse accepted by the Faculty Board prior to or within two days following the exam, they will receive an Incomplete grade of **"I"** in that course. This is conditional upon the student having obtained **at least 60% of the coursework marks** and not having been barred from exams due to absence.
* Students with an "I" grade are permitted to sit for the final exam only. The final grade is computed by combining the score obtained in this final exam with their previously earned coursework marks. The final exam must be taken within the same academic year or the following academic year, otherwise the "I" grade expires, and the student must retake the entire course from scratch.
* If a student submits a compelling excuse within two days of the exam but fails to satisfy the condition of earning 60% of the coursework marks, they will be assigned a withdrawal grade of "W" for the course.

## 10. Discontinuation and Dismissal

### Article (7): Discontinuation of Study
* **A-** A student is considered to have discontinued their studies if they fail to register for a semester or if they withdraw from all courses within a semester without an approved excuse. If the excuse is rejected, the semester is counted against the maximum period allowed for the student to complete their current academic level.
* **B-** A student may interrupt their studies with an acceptable excuse for a maximum of two consecutive semesters or three non-consecutive semesters.
* **C-** Students may submit a formal request to suspend their academic registration in accordance with the rules and guidelines set forth by the University.

### Article (8): Dismissal from the Faculty
A student shall be dismissed from the Faculty based on the failure boundaries stipulated by the executive regulations of the Universities Regulating Law, which specifies the maximum number of years permitted per level as follows:
* **Level 1 Students:** Two years.
* **Level 2 Students:** Two years + one exceptional year subject to Faculty Board approval.
* **Level 3 Students:** Two years + three exceptional years subject to Faculty Board approval.
* **Level 4 Students:** Two years + three exceptional years subject to Faculty Board approval.
* *Note:* Approved suspension periods authorized by the Faculty Board are excluded from the calculation of these maximum allowable years.

## 11. Examination and Evaluation Systems

### Article (9): Examination System
* **A-** The maximum mark for any course is **100 marks**.
* **B-** The minimum passing mark for any course is **50%** of the total course marks, provided the student obtains at least **30%** of the final written exam marks.
* **ج-** Course marks are distributed as follows:
    1. **40% for Coursework**, which is distributed over:
        * Midterm examination with a maximum of 20%.
        * Quizzes, periodic tests conducted by the instructor, practical evaluations, assignments, and optional oral examinations.
    2. **60% for the Final Examination** at the end of the semester.
* The Faculty Board schedules the midterm and final examinations and announces them to students in an appropriate timeframe.
* **D-** If a final exam includes both a written and a practical component based on a departmental proposal approved by the Faculty Board, the final exam score is the sum of both the written and practical exam marks.

### Article (10): Grading System
The Faculty implements the Credit Hours system where the structural unit is the individual course rather than the academic year. Student evaluation is carried out based on the grade point system in each course according to the following framework:

| Score Percentage | Letter Grade | Grade Points | Performance Description |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| 90% and above | A+ | 4.0 | Excellent High |
| 85% - less than 90% | A | 3.7 | Excellent |
| 80% - less than 85% | B+ | 3.3 | Very Good High |
| 75% - less than 80% | B | 3.0 | Very Good |
| 70% - less than 75% | C+ | 2.7 | Good High |
| 65% - less than 70% | C | 2.4 | Good |
| 60% - less than 65% | D+ | 2.2 | Pass High |
| 50% - less than 60% | D | 2.0 | Pass |
| Less than 50% | F | 0.0 | Fail |
| Continuous Course | Con | No Points | The credit hours are not counted toward the GPA until the full completion of the course over the consecutive semester, whether by passing or failing. |

## 12. Calculation of GPA, Graduation, and Honors

* **B-** The Cumulative Grade Point Average (GPA) is computed as follows:
    1. Total points for each course are calculated by multiplying the course's credit hours by the grade points corresponding to the letter grade earned.
    2. Sum the total points accumulated across all courses registered by the student.
    3. Divide the sum of all accumulated points by the total number of credit hours registered to obtain the GPA:

$$\text{GPA} = \frac{\text{Total Quality Points}}{\text{Total Registered Credit Hours}}$$

* **C-** The student's final graduation appreciation rating is determined based on their final cumulative GPA according to the following definitions:

| Cumulative GPA Range | Final Graduation Rating |
| :--- | :--- |
| Less than 1.5 | Very Weak |
| 1.5 - less than 2.0 | Weak |
| 2.0 - less than 2.5 | Pass |
| 2.5 - less than 3.0 | Good |
| 3.0 - less than 3.5 | Very Good |
| 3.5 and above | Excellent |

* **D-** An Honors Degree is awarded if the student maintains a GPA corresponding to no less than "Very Good" in every single academic year of study, provided they complete their graduation requirements within four years, and have never failed or been barred from any course at the Faculty or at any institution from which they transferred.

## 13. Course Repetition and Level Progression

### Article (11): Failing and Repeating
* If a student fails a course (receives an F), they must re-register for the course and take its assessments again. Upon passing the repeated course, the actual mark earned will be recorded up to a maximum cap of **64% (corresponding to a Letter Grade of D)**, and the cumulative GPA will be updated accordingly.

### Article (12): Content Regulations
* **A-** Each academic department creates an integrated course syllabus and specification document for the topics it offers, which is submitted to the Faculty Board. Once approved, these syllabi become binding for all faculty members teaching those courses.
* **B-** Based on proposals from the scientific departments, the Faculty Board may modify the prerequisites or adjust the scientific content of any course, provided the modifications do not exceed **25%** of the originally approved fundamental core content.

### Article (13): Level Progression
A student's academic standing level is verified at the start of each academic year based on the total credit hours successfully completed as follows:
* **Level 1:** The student is placed here upon enrollment and remains at Level 1 as long as they have completed fewer than **27 credit hours**.
* **Level 2:** The student advances to Level 2 upon successfully completing **27 credit hours**.
* **Level 3:** The student advances to Level 3 upon successfully completing **60 credit hours**.
* **Level 4:** The student advances to Level 4 upon successfully completing **96 credit hours**.

#### MA113: Math-2

Sequences and Infinite Series: Tests of convergence and divergence - Alternating series - Power Series.

Harmonic analysis: Real and Complex Fourier Series, Half range and Quarter range expansions. Fourier Integral Transform. Laplace and Inverse Laplace Transforms: Properties and Applications.

* **Prerequisite:** None
* **Contents:** Basic electrical circuits - Coulomb's law - Gauss law, Capacitors - Resistors - Inductors, Kirchhoff's law, Basic circuit theory and circuit analysis - Fundamentals of three phase circuits and transformers, Fundamentals of semiconductor devices - P-N Junction diode - Bipolar junction and field effect transistors structures - Semiconductor devices and circuits - Fundamentals of filters, Power supply and Rectification - Amplifiers - Integrated Circuits and VLSI.

#### ST222: Probability and Statistics-2
* **Prerequisite:** Probability and Statistics-1 (ST121)

* **Contents:** Introduction to Computation and Information; Introduction to Quantum Physics; Computation with Qubits; Periodicity - Quantum Fourier Transform - Kitaev's Phase Estimation Algorithm - Search and Quantum Oracle - Quantum Problem-Solving - Quantum Cognition - General Model of a Quantum Computer - Quantum Walk - Adiabatic Computation - Quantum Neural Computation.

#### AI446: Business Intelligence: Strategies, Tools & Techniques
* **Prerequisite:** Theoretical Foundations of Machine Learning (AI321)

#### AI447: Artificial Intelligence for Cybersecurity
* **Prerequisite:** Supervised Learning (AI322) & Computer Networks Technology (IT221)

#### CS435: Bioinformatics Systems
* **Prerequisite:** Algorithms Analysis and Design (CS321)

#### DS342: Data Analytics
